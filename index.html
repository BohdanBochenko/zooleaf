Cats are clean animals. They lick their fur, and also strive to keep their place in order. But no matter how conscious the mustachioed pets are, they will not cope without our help. Caring for domestic cats begins with a bed, a crib where the animal will sleep. The bed should be in a warm place away from drafts.

If the pet has long hair, then you will have to comb it every day with a special brush. With short hair, the cat is wiped with a wet sponge. You need to wash the animal 2-3 times a year or if necessary. For your convenience, we offer a simple checklist so that you don’t forget anything and provide competent care for your pet.

Feed and drink daily
Clean tray daily
Brush long haired cats daily
Brush your teeth weekly
Trim your nails monthly

Cats don't like a dirty litter box. And if for the owner it can look and smell normal, then it is perceived by the cat as smelly. She may start walking past the tray or marking corners in the house. Cats may be unhappy with stale food and water. Worn food discourages their appetite. If a bowl of water is next to food, the cat is unlikely to drink it. Many pets prefer to lap water straight from the tap or even from the toilet.

Once the cat gives you his love, what absolute certainty, what fidelity of affection! She will be your companion during hours of work, loneliness or sadness.

Bringing a kitten into your home

Before your new pet's fluffy paws walk around the house, take some time to set up his own space, making it a safe, calm and comfortable place from which the kitten can little by little begin to explore the whole house. Settle the kitten in a quiet room, far from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

Playing with your kitten

Playing with a kitten is not only fun, it is essential to help your little one with mental and physical development as well as improve their coordination and social skills.

Grooming for small kittens

In addition to looking beautiful, well-groomed, combed hair contains fewer dead hairs that a cat can swallow during licking, and this activity she spends on average 30% of her time. The dead hairs then enter the cat's digestive system, where they can cause hairballs and digestive problems.

Healthy diet for your kitten

Each stage of a cat's life is characterized by unique nutritional needs that you can meet to keep your pet healthy. Thus, in order to create the best conditions for the normal development of a kitten, his diet must meet the specific needs of the body at certain stages of his life.

Kitten behavior

Kittens can't talk, but they can use a variety of ways to communicate with you. By observing their reactions and movements, as well as listening to the sounds they make, you will learn to understand their desires.
